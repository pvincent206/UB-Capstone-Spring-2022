### Project Updates Week of April 4 This week, we reviewed feedback from the design review. I brought up the decomposition concerns, and we planned to do a crucible test with our own solar salt mixture. Lucy drilled a hole into the brick with the intent that we might use the hole as a way to peek into the brick after the salt has melted and visually confirm a phase change. We also recalculated the heat necessary to warm up the ger. Originally, we were using an estimate based on the chemical energy contained within the coal amount used to heat a ger each day. During the mid review, we were given feedback that this number seemed to be too high, and that about half of this energy is probably lost in smoke. After recalculation using the amount of electricity used to heat a ger, we indeed found that we were overestimating the energy necessary. Instead of 240MJ per day, we estimated that only 120MJ per day is actually needed to heat the ger. Work for this is shown below. This is good news for us, because the last group estimated that their salt brick provided half the heating required to heat a full ger, so with this correct estimation, 1 brick should do the trick. However, we still need to confirm this claim made by the previous group. Next week, we will be reviewing the results of this new insulation casing, and confirming whether or not phase change is happening.
